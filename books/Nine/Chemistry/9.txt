TERM OR PROPERTY NAME: এসিড (Acid)
Bengali Definition: রাসায়নিক দ্রব্যাদির মধ্যে এসিড খুবই গুরুত্বপূর্ণ। এসিড এক ধরনের গুরুত্বপূর্ণ রাসায়নিক দ্রব্য যা পানিতে দ্রবীভূত করলে এসিডের অণু বিয়োজিত হয়ে (ভেঙে) হাইড্রোজেন আয়ন বা প্রোটন (H+) দান করে।
English Translation: Among chemical substances, acid is very important. Acid is an important chemical substance that, when dissolved in water, dissociates (breaks down) into hydrogen ions or protons (H+).

TERM OR PROPERTY NAME: তীব্র এসিড (Strong Acid)
Bengali Definition: HCl ও H₂SO₄ এর ক্ষেত্রে বিয়োজন বোঝাতে একটিমাত্র তীর চিহ্ন ব্যবহার করা হয়েছে। এর অর্থ হলো HCl ও H₂SO₄ পানিতে সম্পূর্ণ (100%) বিয়োজিত হয়। তাই এ ধরনের এসিডকে তীব্র এসিড বা সবল এসিড বলে।
English Translation: In the case of HCl and H₂SO₄, a single arrow is used to indicate dissociation. This means that HCl and H₂SO₄ dissociate completely (100%) in water. Therefore, these types of acids are called strong or potent acids.
Related Chemical Equations: HCl(aq) → H+(aq) + Cl-(aq), H2SO4(aq) → 2H+(aq) + SO42-(aq)

TERM OR PROPERTY NAME: মৃদু এসিড (Weak Acid)
Bengali Definition: অন্য দুইটি এসিড CH3COOH ও H₂CO₃ এর বিয়োজন বোঝাতে উভমুখী তীর চিহ্ন (⇌) ব্যবহার করা হয়েছে। অর্থাৎ এরা পানিতে আংশিক বিয়োজিত হয়। তাই এ ধরনের এসিডকে মৃদু এসিড বা দুর্বল এসিড বলে।
English Translation: The other two acids, CH3COOH and H₂CO₃, use a reversible arrow (⇌) to indicate dissociation. This means they are partially dissociated in water. Thus, these types of acids are called weak acids or mild acids.
Related Chemical Equations: H2CO3(aq) ⇌ 2H+(aq) + CO32-(aq), CH3COOH(aq) ⇌ H+(aq) + CH3COO-(aq)

TERM OR PROPERTY NAME: গাঢ় এসিড (Concentrated Acid)
Bengali Definition: এসিড ও পানির দ্রবণে এসিডের পরিমাণ যদি বেশি থাকে তবে তাকে গাঢ় এসিড বলে।
English Translation: If the amount of acid is high in an acid and water solution, it is called a concentrated acid.

TERM OR PROPERTY NAME: লঘু এসিড (Dilute Acid)
Bengali Definition: আবার, এসিডের জলীয় দ্রবণে পানির পরিমাণ যদি এসিডের তুলনায় অনেক বেশি হয় তবে তাকে লঘু এসিড বলে।
English Translation: If the amount of water is much greater than the amount of acid in an aqueous solution of acid, it is called a dilute acid.

TERM OR PROPERTY NAME: Acid Taste
Bengali Definition: এসিড টক স্বাদযুক্ত।
English Translation: Acids have a sour taste.

TERM OR PROPERTY NAME: লিটমাস পেপার (Litmus Paper)
Bengali Definition: এসিড দ্রবণ নীল রঙের লিটমাস পেপারকে লাল রঙের লিটমাস পেপারে রূপান্তরিত করে।
English Translation: Acid solutions turn blue litmus paper red.

TERM OR PROPERTY NAME: লবণ (Salt)
Bengali Definition: এসিড ধাতব অক্সাইডের সাথে বিক্রিয়া করে লবণ ও পানি উৎপন্ন করে।
English Translation: Acids react with metal oxides to produce salt and water.

LITMUS TEST FOR ACIDS
Purpose: To demonstrate how acids change the color of blue litmus paper.
Materials Required:
  - Test tube
  - 2-3 ml Hydrochloric acid (HCl)
  - Blue litmus paper
Safety Precautions: Wear appropriate lab safety gear. Avoid direct contact with acids, as they can cause burns. Do not taste any chemicals.
Procedure:
 1. Take 2-3 ml of hydrochloric acid in a test tube.
 2. Add a piece of blue litmus paper into the test tube.
Expected Observations: The blue litmus paper will turn red.
Key Learning Points: Acids turn blue litmus paper red.

EXPERIMENT TITLE: সবল ও দুর্বল এসিড অথবা সবল ও দুর্বল ক্ষারের পরীক্ষা / Testing Strong and Weak Acids or Bases
Purpose: To determine which acid is strong and which is weak using a conductivity test.
Materials Required:
    - 50 mL dilute hydrochloric acid (HCl)
    - Two beakers
    - Two graphite rods
    - Connecting wires
    - Battery
    - Light bulb
    - 50 mL ethanoic acid
Safety Precautions:
    - Handle acids with care. Avoid skin contact. Wear safety goggles.
Procedure:
    1. Take 50 mL of dilute hydrochloric acid in a beaker.
    2. Place two graphite rods in the beaker in such a way that they do not touch each other.
    3. Connect one graphite rod to one terminal of the battery and the other graphite rod to the other terminal of the battery through a light bulb.
    4. Observe the brightness of the bulb.
    5. Now, take 50 mL of ethanoic acid in another beaker.
    6. Repeat steps 2 and 3 using the ethanoic acid.
    7. Compare the brightness of the bulb in both cases.
Expected Observations:
    - The bulb will glow in both setups, indicating that both acids conduct electricity.
    - The bulb will glow more brightly in the hydrochloric acid solution than in the ethanoic acid solution.
Key Learning Points:
    - Strong acids like HCl dissociate more completely in water, resulting in a greater concentration of H+ ions.
    - Weak acids like ethanoic acid dissociate less completely, resulting in a lower concentration of H+ ions.
    - The higher the concentration of H+ ions in the solution, the more electricity is conducted, and the brighter the light bulb glows.

9.5 NEUTRALIZATION REACTION
Bengali: 9.5 প্রশমন বিক্রিয়া (Neutralization Reaction)

When these two solutions are mixed, H+ from the acid and OH- from the base react to produce water.

THERMAL POLLUTION OF WATER
If water used for cooling machinery or boilers is released directly into water bodies, it causes thermal pollution. Water temperature can be measured using a thermometer to determine if thermal pollution is present.

WATER PH VALUE
Water with a pH less than 4.5 or greater than 9.5 is not suitable for living organisms. The pH of water can be measured using a pH meter or pH paper.

BOD (BIOLOGICAL OXYGEN DEMAND)
BOD full form is Biological Oxygen Demand. In Bengali it means জৈব রাসায়নিক অক্সিজেন চাহিদা. The amount of oxygen required by microorganisms (like bacteria) to decompose biodegradable organic pollutants in one liter of water is called the BOD of the water. The higher the BOD, the more polluted the water is.

COD (CHEMICAL OXYGEN DEMAND)
COD full form is Chemical Oxygen Demand. In Bengali it means রাসায়নিক অক্সিজেন চাহিদা. The amount of oxygen required to oxidize both organic and inorganic pollutants chemically in one liter of water is called COD of the water. The higher the COD, the more polluted the water is.

BOD AND COD
BOD and COD are both used to measure the level of water pollution. The COD value of any water is higher than the BOD value. This is because BOD only considers the oxygen required to decompose biodegradable organic materials, whereas COD considers the oxygen required to oxidize all organic and inorganic pollutants, whether or not they are biodegradable. Therefore the COD value of the same water will be higher than the BOD value.

WATER PURIFICATION
CHLORINATION
Chlorination: The easiest way to disinfect water is chlorination. Adding the required amount of bleaching powder to water produces chlorine, which kills germs through oxidation. This method is called chlorination of water. In this method, the water becomes drinkable after filtering following the addition of bleaching powder.

BOILING
Boiling: Boiling water for 15 to 20 minutes disinfects it. However, boiling water contaminated with arsenic will make it more harmful.

SEDIMENTATION
Sedimentation: Adding powdered alum (K₂SO₄. Al₂(SO₄)₃. 24H₂O) to a bucket of water and leaving it for half an hour will cause all the impurities to settle to the bottom of the bucket. The clean water can then be poured off from the top, and the insoluble pollutants are removed.

4. এবার ছাঁকন কাগজ আর ফানেলের সাহায্যে মিশ্রণটিকে ছেঁকে নিয়ে একটি টেস্টটিউবে মিশ্রণের জলীয় অংশটুকু নাও। / Now, filter the mixture with filter paper and a funnel, and collect the aqueous part of the mixture into a test tube.
5. টেস্টটিউবের জলীয় অংশে একবার নীল লিটমাস পেপার আর একবার লাল লিটমাস পেপার ডুবাও এবং বর্ণের পরিবর্তন লক্ষ করো। / Dip a blue litmus paper and then a red litmus paper into the aqueous part in the test tube, and note any color change.
EXPECTED OBSERVATIONS:
- If the solution is acidic, the blue litmus paper will turn red.
- If the solution is basic, the red litmus paper will turn blue.
- If the solution is neutral, there will be no color change.
KEY LEARNING POINTS: Litmus paper is an indicator that changes color depending on whether the solution is acidic or basic.

PH PAPER PREPARATION

Take any colorful vegetable (e.g., red spinach, red cabbage, beetroot, etc.) or colorful flower (e.g., hibiscus, red rose, dahlia). Cut it into small pieces and steam it gently. Soak a piece of filter paper in the colored extract you get. Let it air dry and then cut it into thin strips after drying. Your pH paper is ready.

To use this paper, dip it in a solution with an unknown pH and create a color chart for the pH range. Choose the most suitable one for your use.
