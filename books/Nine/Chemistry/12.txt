EXPERIMENTAL PROCEDURES

SALT EXTRACTION FROM SEA WATER (Salt Harvesting)
Purpose: To extract table salt (NaCl) from sea water.
Materials Required: Sea water, salt pans, sunlight, collection container
Safety Precautions: None on this page
Procedure:
    1. Build rectangular or square areas surrounded by dykes at sea coasts.
    2.  Open these areas to allow sea water to flow in during high tides.
    3.  Close the openings, trapping sea water in the pans.
    4. Allow the water to evaporate naturally under the sun.
    5. Collect the solid salt deposits in the pans after the water has dried up.
Expected Observations: Formation of crystalline salt after water evaporation.
Key Learning Points: Salt can be obtained by evaporating sea water.

SALT PURIFICATION
Purpose: To purify salt by removing sand and other impurities
Materials Required: Crude salt, water, filter, container, heater
Safety Precautions: None on this page
Procedure:
    1. Mix the crude salt with water. The salt dissolves, but sand settles.
    2. Filter the solution to remove the sand.
    3. Heat the salt solution to evaporate the water
    4. Collect the salt after all water has evaporated.
Expected Observations: Separation of salt, sand, and water.
Key Learning Points: Salt can be purified by dissolving it in water and then evaporating the water.

BAKING SODA PREPARATION
Bengali Definition: অ্যামোনিয়া গ্যাস, খাদ্যলবণ, পানি এবং কার্বন ডাই-অক্সাইড থেকে বেকিং সোডা প্রস্তুত করা যায়। প্রথমে পানির মধ্যে NaCl কে দ্রবীভূত করে NaCl এর সম্পৃক্ত দ্রবণ প্রস্তুত করা হয়। এবার এই সম্পৃক্ত দ্রবণের মধ্যে NH₃ গ্যাস প্রবাহিত করে NH₃ দ্বারা সম্পৃক্ত করা হয়। কার্বন ডাই-অক্সাইড গ্যাসকে NH₃ সম্পৃক্ত NaCl দ্রবণের মধ্যে প্রবাহিত করা হয়। এক্ষেত্রে CO2, NH3, H₂O একত্র হয়ে প্রথমে অ্যামোনিয়াম হাইড্রোজেন কার্বনেট (NH4HCO₃) উৎপন্ন হয়। এরপর অ্যামোনিয়াম হাইড্রোজেন কার্বনেট সোডিয়াম ক্লোরাইড-এর সাথে বিক্রিয়া করে সোডিয়াম হাইড্রোজেন কার্বনেট (NaHCO3) বা বেকিং সোডা উৎপন্ন করে।
English Translation: Baking soda can be produced from ammonia gas, table salt, water, and carbon dioxide. First, NaCl is dissolved in water to prepare a saturated solution. Then, NH₃ gas is passed through the saturated solution. Carbon dioxide gas is passed through this NH₃-saturated NaCl solution. In this process, CO2, NH3, and H₂O combine to produce ammonium hydrogen carbonate (NH4HCO₃). Then ammonium hydrogen carbonate reacts with sodium chloride to form sodium hydrogen carbonate (NaHCO3), also known as baking soda.
Related Chemical Equations:
CO2 + NH3 + H2O → NH4HCO3
NH4HCO3 + NaCl → NaHCO3 + NH4Cl

EXPERIMENTAL PROCEDURES

SOAP PREPARATION
Purpose: To prepare soap through a saponification reaction
Materials Required: Not explicitly listed, but implied: oil or fat, steam bath, stirring rod, water, sodium chloride (NaCl), filter paper
Safety Precautions: Not explicitly stated, but care should be taken when working with heat, chemicals, and glassware.
Procedure:
    1. Heat the mixture in a steam bath for 30 minutes.
    2. Stir the mixture occasionally with a stirring rod, and add water as needed to replenish evaporated water in the steam bath.
    3. Continue heating until the mixture forms a thick, sticky substance, indicating the oil/fat has saponified.
    4. Stop heating and allow the mixture to cool.
    5. Add 50 mL of saturated NaCl solution to the cooled mixture and let it stand overnight.
    6. The next day, filter the mixture using filter paper, discard the filtrate, and allow the soap to dry.
Expected Observations: The mixture will change from liquid to a thick, sticky substance. After adding NaCl and letting it settle, the solid soap will separate from the liquid.
Key Learning Points: This process demonstrates saponification, the chemical reaction that produces soap from fats/oils. It shows how salt helps in the separation of soap.

SOAP TESTING
Purpose: To assess the properties of prepared and commercially available soaps
Materials Required: Test tubes, prepared soap sample, commercial soap sample, water, kerosene, pH paper.
Safety Precautions: Not explicitly stated, but handle chemicals and glassware carefully.
Procedure:
    1. Take one part water and a sample of the prepared soap in a test tube. Close the test tube mouth and shake well. Observe if foam is produced.
    2. Add 2/3 drops of kerosene to the test tube, shake and observe. Note how kerosene interacts with grease and the result.
    3. Determine the pH of the prepared soap.
    4. Repeat the above three steps with a commercially purchased soap.
    5. Compare and analyze the properties of both soaps.
Expected Observations: Soap should produce foam, emulsify grease (kerosene), and have a certain pH. The homemade and commercial soap will show similar properties.
Key Learning Points: This experiment tests the detergent properties and basicity of soaps.
