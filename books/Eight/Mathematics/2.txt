This actual cost is called investment. This investment is considered the purchase price for determining profit or loss. The price at which the goods are sold is called the selling price. If the selling price is higher than the purchase price, there is a profit. If the selling price is lower than the purchase price, there is a loss. If the purchase price and selling price are equal, there is neither profit nor loss. Profit or loss is calculated based on the purchase price.
Mathematical Notation:

PROBLEM-SOLVING FRAMEWORK
EXAMPLE STRUCTURE
Problem Statement:
- Bengali Version: উদাহরণ ১। একজন দোকানদার প্রতি হালি ডিম ২৫ টাকা দরে ক্রয় করে প্রতি ২ হালি ৫৬ টাকা দরে বিক্রয় করলে তাঁর শতকরা কত লাভ হবে?
- English Translation: Example 1. A shopkeeper buys eggs at 25 taka per hali (4 eggs) and sells 2 halis for 56 taka. What is his profit percentage?
- Given Information: Cost price of 1 hali of eggs = 25 taka, Selling price of 2 halis of eggs = 56 taka
- Unknown Elements: Profit percentage

Solution Path:
1. Understanding Phase
   - Key information identification: Cost price per hali, selling price for 2 halis.
   - Relevant formula selection: Profit = Selling Price - Cost Price, Profit Percentage = (Profit / Cost Price) * 100
   - Strategy planning: Calculate the cost price of 2 halis, find the profit, and then calculate the profit percentage.

2. Solution Steps
   - Cost price of 2 halis = 25 * 2 = 50 taka
   - Profit = 56 - 50 = 6 taka
   - Profit percentage = (6 / 50) * 100 = 12%
   - Mathematical reasoning: The profit is the difference between the selling price and the cost price. The profit percentage is the profit expressed as a percentage of the cost price.
   - Common mistake warnings: Ensure the cost price and selling price are for the same quantity of goods.

3. Answer Verification
   - Method of checking: Recalculate the profit and profit percentage.
   - Alternative approaches: None
   - Units and precision: Percentage is unitless.

EXAMPLE STRUCTURE
Problem Statement:
- Bengali Version: উদাহরণ ২। একটি ছাগল ৮% ক্ষতিতে বিক্রয় করা হলো। ছাগলটি আরও ৮০০ টাকা বেশি মূল্যে বিক্রয় করলে ৮% লাভ হতো। ছাগলটির ক্রয়মূল্য নির্ণয় কর।
- English Translation: Example 2. A goat was sold at an 8% loss. If the goat had been sold for 800 taka more, there would have been an 8% profit. Find the cost price of the goat.
- Given Information: Loss percentage = 8%, Increased selling price = 800 taka, Profit percentage if sold at increased price = 8%
- Unknown Elements: Cost price of the goat

Solution Path:
1. Understanding Phase
   - Key information identification: Loss percentage, profit percentage, and the difference in selling price.
   - Relevant formula selection: Selling price = Cost price * (1 - Loss percentage), Selling price = Cost price * (1 + Profit percentage)
   - Strategy planning: Assume a cost price of 100 taka, calculate the selling prices at 8% loss and 8% profit, find the difference in selling prices, and then use the given difference to find the actual cost price.

2. Solution Steps
   - If cost price is 100 taka, selling price at 8% loss = 100 - 8 = 92 taka
   - If cost price is 100 taka, selling price at 8% profit = 100 + 8 = 108 taka
   - Difference in selling prices = 108 - 92 = 16 taka
   - If the difference in selling price is 16 taka, the cost price is 100 taka.
   - If the difference in selling price is 800 taka, the cost price is (100/16) * 800 = 5000 taka
   - Mathematical reasoning: The difference in selling prices corresponds to the difference between the loss and profit percentages.
   - Common mistake warnings: Ensure the loss and profit percentages are calculated on the same cost price.

3. Answer Verification
   - Method of checking: Recalculate the selling prices at 8% loss and 8% profit using the calculated cost price.
   - Alternative approaches: None
   - Units and precision: Taka
